Brief

One of the most difficult aspects of design to learn to deal with is the design review. A design critique involves a small group of people to discussing a set of designs. The purpose of a design critique is to obtain feedback from those in the room about a set of different design approaches. For example, a representative from the QA (quality assurance) department might raise testing issues, developers might ask feasibility questions, marketing folks might ask questions about advertising, a creative director might comment on the visual direction.

For a beginning designer, this review can be emotionally trying. Always remember that the ultimate goal is to improve the work. Despite the initial sting, you need honest feedback to become a better designer. It is never meant to be personal. The purpose of this card is to review some things to remember when receiving and giving a critique.

Rules for a good critique

  1. Review the goal(s) of the project
  2. Identify any constraints and requirements
  3. Allow the design to be completely presented before raising any questions or comments

  4. Keep comments constructive and actionable

    • All of your feedback must follow this simple acronym: ASK (Actionable, Specific, Kind)
    • Actionable: your feedback must be clear enough that the recipient can make positive changes to their work to make it improve.
    • Specific: you must avoid vague feedback that speaks in only generalities. You must be very direct and point exactly where something isn't working, and articulate why.
    • Kind: Kindness is not niceness. Kindness is loving and fearless. Kindness is respecting your fellow classmate to give them honest critiques of their work.

Things to keep in mind when receiving critiques

  1. Be humble - understand that you have things to learn and accept that your work is not perfect
  2. Keep an open mind
  3. Don't take the feedback personally
  4. Take notes
  5. Listen before speaking
  6. Dig deeper when necessary in order to understand the reasons for the comments in order to learn how to improve the work
  7. Be positive and polite
  8. Thank the critic
