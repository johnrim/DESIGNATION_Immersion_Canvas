Introduction

The design sprint is a process for prototyping and testing any product in 5 days. Design-thinking compressed into a short time-frame:

Define -> Research -> Ideation -> Prototype -> Choose -> Implement -> Learn

  • Day 1: [Understand](http://www.gv.com/lib/the-product-design-sprint-understandday-1) Dig into the design problem through research, competitive review, and strategy exercises.
  • Day 2: Diverge: Rapidly develop as many solutions as possible.
  • Day 3: [Decide](http://www.gv.com/lib/the-product-design-sprint-decideday3) Choose the best ideas and hammer out a user story.
  • Day 4: [Prototype](http://www.gv.com/lib/the-product-design-sprint-prototypeday4) Build something quick and dirty that can be shown to users.
  • Day 5: [Validate](http://www.gv.com/lib/the-product-design-sprint-validateday5) Show the prototype to real humans (in other words, people outside your company) and learn what works and what doesnâ€™t work.
