Design Process

  1. Set a schedule and deadline(s)
    • Be realistic! Don't be overly optimistic about your capabilities.
  2. Figure out who you're designing the product for
    • User personas are extremely helpful for identifying your target audience
    • Create use cases for these potential users: what is their purpose on the site, what sort of information are they looking for, etc.
  3. Develop and understand the content first
    • Form follows function!
  4. Create a moodboard or inspiration board
  5. Start with a sketch
    • Don't jump right into Photoshop or Illustrator!
    • The sketch can be a high-fidelity wireframe or something more loose
  6. Create a grid first!
    • If you're using bootstrap, this would be a 12 column grid
  7. Develop a unifying style guide
    • Choose the typography
    • Identify the type hierarchy
    • Define the color palette
    • Define the visual styling for specific content types
  8. Finish a rough draft before trying to throw something away and start over
    • It's easier to iterate once you have the outline of a complete website finished, even if it's ugly. You'll start to see a larger pattern develop in your mind when you complete the whole thought.
  9. Give yourself enough time to reflect on your own work
    • This is my 24 hour rule. You need to have enough time to sleep on your design and look at it again in the morning with a fresh pair of eyes.
    • It's hard to be objective about one's own work in the moment.
  10. Share your work with peers
    • Try to illicit feedback from fellow designers who may offer constructive criticism.
  11. Iterate!
    • A design is rarely finished with its first pass. Keep refining and polishing the design
