Negative Space

White space also referred to as negative space is one means of creating mood and meaning. Negative space is, quite simply, the space that surrounds an object in a image. Just as important as that object itself, negative space helps to define the boundaries of positive space and brings balance to a composition.

Negative space does not have to be white, or even solid in color. It can contain gradients, patterns or background objects. It is the space between specific items on the page.

  • Negative space can be used to give more significance to a piece of text or image. You can use negative space in a poster or on a logo, etc.
  • Negative space can be used cleverly to tell a story
  • Negative space can be used expressively with asymmetrical design
  • Active white space can be used to imply movement
  • Ideas that empty space can signify include:
  • Quality: extravagance
  • Solitude: loneliness
  • Something missing: lost, stolen, misplaced
  • Clean: bleached, washed
  • Purity: unsullied, virgin, unadulterated
  • Heaven: absolution, sacredness
  • Abundance: plenty, vastness
  • Openness: distance, infinity
  • Calmness: tranquility
  • Ice: snow, sky, day, milk

Grid/Layout

What is a Grid System?

...a structure comprising a series of horizontal and vertical lines, used to arrange content.

  • Begin by choosing a grid structure. If you're using Bootstrap, it should be a 12 column grid.
  • Flow is important to any website because you want your user to reach the goal you set out for them quickly and easily. The faster the better.
  • Whether words or images, visitors naturally read content from left to right and from top to bottom.
  • Visual movement will help the user to make decisions and perform complex functions with little direct instruction.
  • Design 101 tip: People look at the eyes first on a human being. Construct the content around where you want them to look first, and put the relevant content around there.
  • Establish hierarchy with white space
  • Create hierarchy with sizes: the larger the text or image, the more it will draw attention.
