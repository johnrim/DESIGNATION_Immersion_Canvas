7 Parts of a Well-crafted Research Plan

  1. Project Context: The client's business problem
  2. Objectives: Questions and gaps in knowledge
  3. Audience: Demographics and psychographics
  4. Locations: Where the research will take place
  5. Methods: Activities that help you fill the gaps
  6. Schedule: When the activities will happen
  7. Outcomes: What deliverables are generated

Basic Research Outline

  1. Define the user groups: If you don’t know who your users are, then this is one of the first things you should plan to research.
  2. Plan to test the appropriate groups: Groups can be based on demographics, experiences, roles, primary goals, etc. Personas help create cohesion among these dimensions and create a focus on behavior.
  3. Create and share your research plan
  4. Conduct research
  5. Synthesize research with team members and stakeholders
  6. Create and share prioritized recommendations and requirements
