Objectives & Outline

  1. Understand what a wireframe is
  2. Learn how to create a wireframe
  3. Make a series of wireframes

Brief:

A wireframe is a tool that is used to visualize your experience before going into high fidelity designs. They are typically a low-fidelity, bare-bones blueprint, usually represented with gray boxes and placeholders for detailed content. Their goal is to help establish what goes where, without consuming too much time on aesthetics just yet. It allows designers to experiment and work out visual problems before wasting a second on pushing pixels.

A good wireframe should explain:

  • how the content is organized
  • what pieces of information should exist on a given page or module
  • how the information is structured
  • the most basic visuals involved in the UI interaction

Additionally, a wireframe is a communication tool to better coordinate with different stakeholders, be it a client or a member of the development team. The wireframes provide documentation for the team on how the website should be designed.

In-class Exercise

  • Conduct hierarchical task analysis
  • Using Illustrator, Omnigraffle, Sketch, or Axure, create mobile UI wireframes
  • Spend some time to create lo-fidelity wireframe (15 minutes)
  • Work within teams to share ideas on the wireframes (15-30 minutes)
  • Individually create mid-fidelity wireframes for 4-5 screens (15-30 minutes)
  • Prepare to share your design with the class and explain
  • Use Guide to Wireframing for more in-detail (reference Page 25 for '4 Non-Digital Wireframing Weapons')
