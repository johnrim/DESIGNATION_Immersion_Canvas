What is a Microinteraction?

Microinteractions are contained product moments that revolve around a single use caseâ€”they have one main task. Microinteractions are small pieces of functionality that encapsulate a specific user interaction.

Microinteractions are good for:

  • accomplishing a single task
  • connecting devices together
  • adjusting a setting

Four elements of a microinteraction

  1. Triggers
  2. Rules
  3. Feedback
  4. Loops & Modes
