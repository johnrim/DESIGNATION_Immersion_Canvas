Display Type

There are two kinds of type: display and text. Text is where the story is. Display is there to describe the content and to draw the reader in.

Two kinds of display type: primary and secondary.

  • Primary type: usually a headline
  • Secondary type: also known as the subhead, usually explains the headline

Headline treatments fall into one of 3 categories:

  • alignment and position
  • contrasting type styles
  • integration of type and imagery

Regardless of design treatment, a great headline is provocatively written and makes an immediate point.

Secondary type:

  • should be less prominent than headline
  • usually this means the font size is smaller, lighter in color or positioned differently with different spacing.

Typography Fundamentals for Web Design

  1. When pairing fonts, strive for contrast over harmony.
    • Pair serif fonts with sans-serif
    • Pair a humanist serif with a humanist sans-serif
    • Pair a script font with serif
    • Pair a geometric sans-serif with a serif
  2. Choose at most 2-3 fonts
  3. Choose a body font size of 16-26px
  4. Choose a leading of roughly 120-150% of the body font
  5. Create a visual hierarchy
    • Headlines should have be roughly 300% the size of the body copy
    • Subheads should be roughly 150%
    • Use font weights, bolding, and italics to add more visual contrast and draw attention to the most important elements on a page
  6. Limit the characters per line in a paragraph to 50-70 for desktop, 30-40 for mobile
  7. Avoid widows and orphans in block text
  8. Avoid goofy fonts like Comic Sans, mono­spaced fonts, and sys­tem fonts, es­pe­cially Times New Ro­man and Arial.
  9. Use curly quo­ta­tion marks, not straight ones.
  10. Put only one space be­tween sen­tences.
  11. Don’t use mul­ti­ple word spaces or other white-space char­ac­ters in a row.
  12. Never use un­der­lin­ing, un­less it’s a hyperlink.
