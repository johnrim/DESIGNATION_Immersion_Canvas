Purpose of Exploratory Research

  1. Explore domains
  2. Acquire empathy
  3. Find insights
  4. Test assumptions
  5. Build hypotheses

Research Methods

  1. Qualitative: Studies that are qualitative in nature generate data about behaviors or attitudes based on observing them directly.
    • User interviews
      • Semi-structured
      • Contextual inquiry
    • Observational research
      • Ethnography
    • Competitive analysis
    • Stakeholder interviews
    • Subject Matter Expert (SME) interviews
    • Focus Group
  2. Quantitative: Quantitative studies the data about the behavior or attitudes in question are gathered indirectly, through a measurement or an instrument such as a survey or an analytics tool.
    • Market research
    • Surveys
    • Analytics

Attitudinal vs Behaviorial Research

  1. Attitudinal: The purpose of attitudinal research is to understand or measure people's stated beliefs.
  2. Behaviorial: Behaviorial research methods focus mostly on understanding "what people do" with the product or service in question
    • A/B testing
    • Eye tracking
    • Mouse tracking
    • Web Analytics

3 Broad Kinds of Research

  1. Exploratory: Focuses on finding insights and opportunities through observing people. It tends to focus on how people behave, where they interact, and their current day perceptions.
  2. Concept Validation: Focuses on gathering feedback (data) on whether a design, idea, or strategy is effective. It tends to involve getting feedback from people on a new concept, feature, design via a prototype.
  3. Usability: Focuses on gathering feedback (generally qualitative data) directly from people on whether an existing or proposed design meets their needs in terms of functionality, simplicity, and accessibility.

User Interviews Best Practices

  1. Practice active listening
    • Don't interrupt
    • Take notes
    • Dig deeper
  2. Use open-ended questions
    • Ask who, what, when, where, why, and how
    • Avoid close-ended questions
    • Avoid leading questions
  3. Paraphrase what you're hearing
  4. Make the participant feel comfortable
