Brief

The promise of a fully connected home has finally arrived. With the emergence of the Internet of Things (IoT), users can now control everything in their home through remotely. People can control their home's temperature (NEST), they can control their lights, their entertainment, and much more. See the attached pdf on the smart home vision.

Vision of Smart Home

However, the landscape of these controllable devices is still very fragmented and there isn't an integrated way for users to control their whole home. For this project, you must create an interface for a fictitious platform that allows users to control everything through a single interface.

This platform can integrate everything from home entertainment to home security. The specific list of controllable areas are as follows:

  • Utilities:
    • Monitor electricity
    • Monitor water usage
    • Report on solar power
  • Security:
    • Manage front door, garage door
    • Manage security cameras
    • Carbon monoxide and smoke detector
  • Environment:
    • Manage lights
    • Control cooling and heating through the thermostat
  • Entertainment:
    • Music
    • Radio
    • Television
    • Movies

This platform can be accessed from the web or through mobile devices like the phone or tablet. The platform is adaptive and can learn users' preferences and automatically set the environment to accommodate.

The Challenge

For the next 6 weeks, you will need to come up with a vision for the software that will allow users to control their smart home ecosystem.

  • You will be required to conduct research, create wireframes, and produce high-fidelity designs.
  • At the end of the first 3 weeks, your team must present your research and UX findings and interaction model in the form of wireframes and a prototype.
  • In the final 3 weeks, your team will present a high-fidelity prototype of your proposed solution with branding and visual design.

Class Presentations

Once the project is complete, you will be presenting your work to a room of professionals as a team.

  1. Each team will have 15-20 minutes *(max)* for your presentation
  2. Your presentation should be created using either Keynote, Google Slides, or Powerpoint
  3. There will be 10-15 minutes for questions and feedback
  4. Presentations will start promptly
  5. Each team member will need to participate in the presentation
